NY's best districts to live in for a NYU Stern School of Business student

Introduction.

This is a web application which will allow you to rank NY's districts according to several different factors of huge relevance in order to enjoy a pleasant stay at NY.

The factors in consideration are the following:
  • Safety - Measured by the number and severity of the crimes committed in a given district in December 31st, 2015.
  • Affordability - Given by the number of low income units attributed to a given district. Such number, by the way, means nothing but the amount of units with rents that are affordable to households earning 51 to 80% of the area median income.
  • Closeness - Taken as the distance of the district's centre to the NYU Stern School of business.
  • Air quality - Determined as the result of pondering traffic density, related respiratory, cardiovascular and asthma hospitalizations, deaths and ED (i.e. Emergency Department) visits, and air contaminant substances emissions and concentrations.
  • Leisure - Quantified by pondering the number of farmer markets, nearby fishing rivers, child care programs covering the district, authorized liquor expediters, museums and art galleries.
  • Transportation - Assessed by considering the number of subway stations, the typical usage given to the public transportation system (public transportation services congestion) and the particular vehicles operation intensity.
The sources for the data involved in the computation of the previously mentioned indicators are accessible via the following links:
  • Neighborhood Names GIS.
  • NY Districts geoshapes.
  • Crimes in NY.
  • Dataset contains information on New York City housing by building data.
  • Dataset contains information on New York City air quality surveillance data.
  • New York City Museums.
  • New York City Art Galleries.
  • New York City Farmers Markets.
  • Open.ny.gov API Catalog.
The service will be provided in the following way:
  • You count with the possibility to obtain certain information related to every habitable district of NY. Such information is a valuation of the district for each of the previously listed factors and their subfactors (i.e. The indicators summing up a factor, as described above for each of the factors), plus a geographical reference to the district with respect to the other districts (Google Maps). The valuations will be displayed both in a singular and comparative way, so that it is possible to compare the district's habitability with that one of the other districts.
    All you have to do is to enter the district of interest's name in the text box displayed right after clicking on the "district search" option, which lays in the navigation bar, at the beginning of the page.
  • For a faster access to potentially relevant information, you can access the list of the most habitable districts according to some criteria (normally a single factor, sometimes even three). The information displayed for each district will be the same as in the last item.
    All what needs to be done to access this service is to click on the option of interest right above, in the navigation bar.
